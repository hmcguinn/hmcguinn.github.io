Research Interests

Broadly, I'm interested in the intersection of security and software engineering.

Security has always fascinated me. I read Bruce Schneier's Applied Cryptography in high school and was hooked on the field. My first professional exposure was last summer as an intern at Sandia National Laboratories. While there, I worked on Robohunter: a machine learning framework for batch network analytics.

One of my current projects is RTLSDR RNG, a wrapper for Python's Random class using entropy from a software-defined radio as the seed. Another project I'm working on is expanding GLTR (Giant Language model Test Room) to detect machine generated text. It's currently in the very early stages.

Colors and colorblindness!

I'm a deutan! Colorblindness is really interesting to me; we each have our individual, subjective experience of the world. It's also made me more aware of accessibility issues.
